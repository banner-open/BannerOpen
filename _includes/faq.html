Banner Open Rules

  • Alcohol

    Alcohol must be 2 units or a standard drink, for example, a 4% can of 500ml lager is 2 units. If using a spirit or higher beer % the unit will have to be calculated and verified before the athlete can play. Day 1 - 6 cans (12 units) each worth a point (Min 5 cans/10 units to be consumed).
    Day 1 - 6 cans (12 units) each worth a point (Min 5 cans/10 units to be consumed)
    Day 2 - 27 cans* per team (9x3), 8 cans* worth 0.75 points and final can* worth 1 point. Cans can be distributed among teammates as the team sees fit once each player has drank the 5 can* minimum. *cans or 2 unit equivalent.
    There is a 2 point penalty per can if the 5 can minimum is not met, a further one point penalty for vomiting. Cans must be collected, quantities verified and brought back after the round.

  • Scoring

    Non golfers to play off 28. Golfers with a GUI to play their handicap.

    Day 1 - Singles play off their handicaps. Two ball scramble teams play off 18 Handicaps (Because Pa does not believe in yer ability to play of 13 and 14 respectively).

    Day 2 - Three ball scramble (calculated at 35% of Player A and 25% of player Bs and 15% of Player Cs handicap).

  • Golf Rules and Etiquette

    Play Ready Golf. 4 golfers should finish 9 holes around the 2 hour mark and the full 18 in around 4 hours. Try to play and drink to this pace.

    Do not walk across someones line on the putting green. If you have scratched a hole and cannot score on that hole, pick up your ball, drink and move on to the next hole rather than delay and waste time hitting meaningless shots.

    White stakes and Out of Bounds: Out-of-bounds brings the dreaded stroke-and-distance penalty: a golfer must apply a one-stroke penalty, return to the spot of the previous shot and hit it again.

    Yellow Stakes: The penalty is one stroke. There are two options for putting a new ball into play. One is to return to the spot from which the previous stroke was played and hit it again. The second, and more commonly chosen option, is to take a drop on the line it came in.

    Red Stakes: Indicate a lateral water hazard. The golfer will apply the one-stroke penalty and take a drop. The drop can be taken within two club-lengths of the point where the ball entered the hazard, no nearer the hole. Or a golfer can go to the opposite side of the hazard and drop at a spot on the hazard's margin that is equidistant from the hole.

    Bunkers: You cannot ground your club in the sand, or take practice shots behind your ball if it will interfere with the sand and improve your lie. Doing so incurs a one stroke penalty. You can move your ball in the bunker 6 inches and no nearer the hole as rakes are out of use due to Covid.
